Culture Frame

“Before we dive in, I want to set a quick frame.”

“Brad is big on culture. He only wants to work with people who are grateful, accountable, coachable, and driven — people who are serious about leveling up, not just curious.”

“So as we go through this, I’m listening for those things in you — how you think, how you respond, and whether this really lines up with where you want to go.”

“My job is simple: if it’s a fit, I’ll show you exactly how to plug in. If it’s not, we’ll shake hands and be honest about that too.”

Quick Interview Setup

“Cool. I’m going to ask you a few quick questions so I understand where you’re starting from and what you’re aiming for.”

“There are no right or wrong answers — I just want the real picture so I can tell you honestly whether this is the right move for you.”

“Sound good?”

Discovery Questions

“First one — where are you calling in from?”

“Are you familiar with Brad Lea and his content?”

If yes, ask: “Nice. What have you seen from him so far?”
If no, tap the Companies button later and briefly frame Brad / ecosystem when needed.

“How long have you been doing that?”

“What kind of sales experience do you have?”

“Were you closing over the phone, in person, or both? And what were you selling, at what price point?”

“Have you earned commissions before?”

“What was your best year in commission-based income?”

“What are you going to do with the money when you start making more? Is it paying off debt, investing back into a business, taking care of family — what’s the mission for you?”

“Do you do anything right now for personal development — reading, training, fitness, gratitude, anything like that?”

mentally tag as a strong future placement candidate for direct referral.

Reflect & Align

“Alright, based on everything you’ve shared, here’s what I’m seeing…”

This is where YOU speak — mirror back 2–3 strengths, lightly mention one growth area.

“What I’m really looking for is gratitude, accountability, coachability, and real drive — and I’m getting a clear sense of that from you.”

“Let me show you what Brad built, and how the system actually works.”

Brad & Ecosystem Snapshot

“Training high-level closers — that’s the mission here.”

“Brad is a master salesman and one of the most documented voices in the sales space. He’s worked with names like Tony Robbins, Grant Cardone, John Maxwell, Les Brown, Zig Ziglar, Ed Mylett — and brands like General Motors, the UFC, Top Chef, and a long list of franchises and training companies.”

“On top of that, Brad owns multiple companies across software, financial services, events, coaching, and sales training. Those are the vehicles we plug people into once they’re trained and producing.”

“Real Training is the engine he built so people can learn the exact mindset, skill set, and daily habits that helped him become ultra-successful through sales — then apply it in their own life and career.”

If they don’t really know Brad, keep this card open a bit longer and slow down. You can always tap the Companies button later to give more detail on the ecosystem.

The Three Pillars

“Real Training is built around three pillars: mindset, skill set, and habits.”

“Mindset is how Brad thinks about deals, money, and people — how he frames opportunity, handles rejection, and keeps his head right.”

“Skill set is the fundamentals and advanced closing techniques — how to open, qualify, frame, present, handle objections, and close without being weird or pushy.”

“Habits are the daily routines and standards that keep production consistent — how you structure your day, track your numbers, and stay accountable.”

“The goal isn’t to just give you information. The goal is to turn you into a producer.”

Training & Placement

“Once you’re trained and producing, there are multiple companies in Brad’s ecosystem where performers can be placed — depending on fit and timing.”

“When you join Real Training and complete the program, you’re guaranteed placement into one of Brad’s core companies. Next year there will also be opportunities with partner companies that plug into the same system.”

“This isn’t ‘here’s a course, good luck.’ The point is to train you, then plug you into a real vehicle where you can use the skills.”

You can tap Companies at any point if they want details about LightSpeed VT, Real Financial, Real Merchant, Real Coach, Real Success Events, Dropping Bombs, Closer School, or Real Training itself.

$5,000 Commitment Deposit

“To join Real Training, there’s a $5,000 commitment deposit.”

“I call it a commitment deposit on purpose — it’s not a random fee. It’s skin in the game so you take this seriously and actually finish.”

“The $5,000 can be structured a few ways — paid in full, split, or financed — but the agreement is the same.”

“You complete Real Training, you onboard into one of Brad’s companies and complete their training, and then you help that company generate $50,000 in revenue.”

“When that happens, your full $5,000 commitment deposit is refunded back to you. So if you execute, the training ends up costing you nothing.”

“You’re not betting on Brad — he’s already built this. You’re betting on yourself.”

Why There’s a Deposit

“Brad used to give the training away for free.”

“What he found was — people didn’t show up, didn’t finish, and quit on themselves. Meanwhile he’s paying coaches and constantly upgrading the system.”

“So he changed the model. Now there’s skin in the game. The $5,000 comes back when you do your part.”

“If you don’t give up, we won’t give up on you.”

Primary Close

“Based on everything we’ve covered — your background, what you want to earn, how the program and placement works…”

“…does it make sense for you to get started with Real Training and bet on yourself today?”

Handle Objections

“Totally fair — most people have at least one question or concern before they lock it in.”

“Is it more about the money, the timing, talking to someone else, or wondering if this will actually work for you?”

Use the Objections button at the top right to pull up specific objection cards while they talk: Money, Think About It, Spouse, What If It Doesn’t Work, etc.

Objection – Money Is Tight

“I get it — totally fair.”

“The way I look at it, if you plan on producing $50,000 anyway, this money is coming back to you.”

“The real question isn’t ‘can you afford $5,000?’ The real question is — do you believe you’ll execute and follow through to the point where you earn at least $50,000?”

Objection – “I Need to Think About It”

“I respect that — thinking is good.”

“Usually when someone says they need to think about it, it means there’s one or two things that aren’t totally clear yet.”

“What specifically are you unsure about, or what information do you still need to feel 100% solid?”

Objection – “I Need to Talk to My Spouse”

“Totally understand — they should be in the loop.”

“When you talk to them, what’s your plan for how you’re going to explain why you want to do this, and what it could do for your income and your future?”

“Money aside, is this something you want to move forward with?”

Objection – “What If It Doesn’t Work?”

“Great question.”

“The way this doesn’t work is if you don’t show up or you quit.”

“If you don’t give up, we won’t give up on you. Once you complete the training, get placed, and help generate $50,000 in revenue, your commitment deposit comes back to you.”

How Payment Works

“If we move forward, I’ll send you a secure payment link with a few options.”

“You’ll choose whether you want to structure the $5,000 as paid in full, split, or financed.”

“There’s also a $1,500 option that doesn’t include the refund — that’s for people who see the value in the training and are willing to spend the money either way. The payments on that can be well under $100 a month, and a lot of people will keep their current role while they ramp this up part time.”

You’ll send the actual link outside the app — this card is just your teleprompter.

Refund Logic (Clearly)

“Just so we’re 100% clear…”

“The full $5,000 commitment deposit is refundable once you complete Real Training, complete the company’s onboarding after placement, and generate at least $50,000 in revenue with that company.”

“The $1,500 option is the same training and the same access — the only difference is it’s not refundable. That option is usually best for people who just want the skills and are comfortable spending the $1,500 no matter what.”

This May Not Be for Everyone

“This isn’t for everyone — and that’s okay.”

“If you’re unsure, I’d rather you hold off and come back when you’re truly ready. The last thing I want is for you to sign up for something you’ll regret or half-do.”

“But if you’re looking at this thinking, ‘If I stick with it, this could seriously upgrade my life,’ then this is exactly who it’s built for.”

“The best salespeople aren’t in the business of convincing people who are unsure. They’re in the business of helping the right people get what they want — and when that happens, everybody wins.”

Final Check-In

“So with everything we’ve covered — the training, the placement, the refund, and the options…”

“Are you ready to bet on yourself and lock this in?”

If They’re Ready

“Perfect. Here’s what we’ll do.”

“I’ll send you the payment link with the options we talked about. Once that’s handled, you’ll get an email with your login and onboarding instructions.”

“Start with the first modules right away. The faster you implement, the faster you move toward placement and that $50,000 revenue mark.”

“If you have questions as you go, reach out. We’re here to help — I’m excited to see what you do with this.”

If They’re Not Ready

“No worries — like I said, this isn’t for everyone and not everyone is ready right now.”

“I’d rather you wait and come back when you’re fully committed than jump in halfway and second-guess it.”

“In the meantime, keep sharpening your skills, stack some cash, and when you’re ready to really bet on yourself, we can revisit this.”

$1,500 Option (For Pure Training)

“Before we wrap, I do want you to know there’s also the $1,500 option.”

“It’s the same training, the same content, and the same access — the only difference is that it’s not refundable.”

“That’s usually better for people who simply want the skills, don’t need the deposit back, and like the idea of spreading out smaller payments — sometimes under $100 a month — while they keep working their current role.”

Send Payment Link

“If you’re ready, we’ll pick the option that makes the most sense, I’ll send the link, and we’ll get you moving.”
